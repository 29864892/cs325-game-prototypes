The hardest part was making sure that the buttons didn't overlap and that the prompts would actually show up before the game ends. Creating the base ui will definitely make the further iterations of the game easier and allow me to put more effort into other parts of the game.

  • Prototype Goal: What game mechanic is this prototype evaluating? The goal of this prototype was to get the basic ui established and to begin to work on the combat system.
  • Player Experience Goals: What experience do you want players to have when playing your game? I want players to learn the skills through gameplay, and learn what skills work best in order to win.
  • Gameplay: The core gameplay revolves around attacking enemies by clicking which attack to use. There are four options for the given character: charge, which increases attack and ends the game after 15 uses, customer service, which heals the player back to full the first use with diminishing returns, smart punch, which is a basic damage attack, and wrong number, which prevents the enemy from attacking and reduces their damage. Some plans for increasing the difficulty would be to add in status conditions in future fights that restrict these skills (i.e. wrong number and charge have no effect, or customer service healing is halved.).
  • Strategies: What player strategies do you expect will be effective at playing this game? For the first level, spamming charge and healing is the fastest strategy and I plans on punishing this strategy in later levels to force the player to adapt. A more tactical strategy is to use charge to boost smart punch damage. Another abusive strategy might be to spam wrong number for a large amount of turns so that the ai does no damage. This strategy is somewhat addressed in the enemy gaining a set amount of damage each turn, and will be addressed in later levels as well with a limit to effective uses.
